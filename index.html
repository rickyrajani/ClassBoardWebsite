Hello, Class of 2018

We're the Class Board of 2018. Our purpose is to provide programming and to facilitate the University’s greatest traditions in order to instill pride in our community and unity among our classmates. As a member of Penn Student Government, the Class Executive Board represents the voice of their respective classes to the University and its community.

Meet the Team

Vadim

President

When our resident celeb isn’t frequenting Santa Barbara film festivals or meeting stars at Ordovsky-Tanaevsky-produced movies (Ordovsky-Tanaevsky? Still wondering how that fit into the little boxes on standardized tests), you will probably spot him on Locust. He’s the one in a tailored suit or classy-but-casual-outfit from Zara (probably with the top button of his shirt undone) or nothing but a wet towel (s/o to his stellar performance at the Quadramics production this fall), shouting into a microphone, working the crowd. Vaddy’s British accent and dimples charm their way into the hearts of anyone he meets. He is constantly on the phone speaking in multiple languages. Is he Spanish? Russian? British? The world may never know (But actually though, they won’t--the Russian mafia has been instructed to make sure nobody finds out.) Possibly the only freshman with his own business card, Vadim is always busy doing something—he has quite the packed “schedule” (pronounced: shed-yool). Yet somehow this social butterfly is always down for a friendly chat or trip to his favorite restaurant, KGB Monday’s—whoops, sorry--T.G.I Friday’s. Be sure to order the “Vadim special” next time you’re there.

The mortar that holds together the brick of the house. The string that holds together the pearls on a necklace. The polar bonds that cause surface tension in water. Medha

Creative Director

Medha hails from sunny and jovial Wexford PA, and before that, a place with even better weather—London, England. As the funny one, she spends most of her waking hours (because she is secretly a vampire (pre-med) and nocturnal) at rehearsal for Bloomers Comedy Troupe, or laughing at her own puns (at least her mom thinks she’s funny). She once played fiddle in a bluegrass band, owned a pet squirrel named Squirry, can do several accents, and can identify any dog's breed on the spot. When she was younger, her life's aspirations were to host her own talk show, or become Steve Irwin, crocodile hunter, sans sting ray sticking out from the chest, or become an eye surgeon. Jury’s out on whether she will end up curing her patients with humor. After all, seven days without a pun makes one weak (week).
